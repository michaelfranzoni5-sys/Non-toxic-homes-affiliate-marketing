## Trust-Building Strategy

1. **Authority credential everywhere** — "author, anthropologist" appears on X bio, YouTube channel description, Kajabi site, Facebook. The credential is the FIRST thing you see.
2. **Multi-platform consistency** — Same bio, same value prop across all platforms
3. **Research-backed content** — Keywords include "health optimization," "longevity," "anti-aging" — evidence-forward positioning
4. **Community layer** — Skool community creates social proof and recurring revenue
5. **"Protocol" framing** — Doesn't say "information" or "guides," says "protocols" — sounds more premium, actionable, scientific

## Content Formats

- **Long-form YouTube videos** (1,349 videos since 2013) — deep dives on fasting, autophagy, longevity
- **X posts** — Curiosity hooks like "The man who defies aging: Chuando Tan is a 58-year-old man who looks like he's in his 20s..."
- **Books** — Amazon best-sellers establish credibility
- **Kajabi blog** — Full-stack content platform with courses/products
- **Lead magnet** — "Get health %-ile assessment" (Skool community funnel)

## Monetization Model

1. **Free content** (YouTube, X, blog) → **Paid community** (Skool, $/month) → **Protocols & courses**
2. Repeatable: Content builds authority → Authority drives community signups → Community generates recurring revenue
3. No ads, no affiliate focus — direct-to-consumer community model

## Key Insights for Temple Keep

1. **Protocol framing > Guide framing** — "Non-Toxic Home Protocol" sounds more actionable than "Priority Roadmap"
2. **Authority credentials** — Temple Keep needs a "How We Research" / "Methodology" section to establish institutional authority
3. **Lead magnet missing** — Every Siim Land bio points to his Skool community; Temple Keep has no email capture
4. **Curiosity hooks** — Siim Land's X posts use "The man who defies aging" style hooks; Temple Keep's article titles are descriptive but not curiosity-driven
5. **Longevity bridge** — Siim Land connects non-toxic living directly to lifespan extension; Temple Keep mentions "longer life" but the articles are mostly product reviews

## Applied Action

Added "How We Research & Test" methodology section to the homepage and cornerstone roadmap article — directly modeled on Siim Land's persistent authority signaling. Includes sourcing standards, evidence criteria, and independence guarantee. This fills the biggest gap: zero trust/authority signals on Temple Keep.
