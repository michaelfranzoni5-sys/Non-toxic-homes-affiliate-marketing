## 1. Core Philosophy

> **"Be a knowledgeable friend helping another person, not a content farm producing pages."**

Every decision flows from this. The site should feel like one person who genuinely researched this topic and wants to help you make the right choice. Not a generic review site.

### The Universal Affiliate Blueprint (from Clever Hiker audit)

1. **Quantified credibility** — use specific numbers (tested X products, researched Y hours, compared Z criteria)
2. **Human proof** — original photos, author bylines, personal experience, named team members
3. **Transparent methodology** — explain how products were selected, tested, and scored
4. **Decision segmentation** — organize by buyer intent (budget, feature, use case), not by brand
5. **Clear next step** — make affiliate buttons obvious, never bury links
6. **Education-first** — teach the reader before selling them
7. **Freshness signals** — update dates, editor's notes, "last updated" callouts
8. **No AI content feel** — write like a human, use personal anecdotes, avoid generic language

## 2. Content Strategy

### Article Structure (Exact Template)

Every review article must follow this structure in order:

1. **H1:** "Best [Product] for [Use Case] — [Year]"
2. **Intro paragraph** (2-3 sentences) — hook, problem, promise
3. **How We Tested** — 2-3 sentence methodology box with quantified claims
4. **Quick Picks** — Numbered table of top picks with "Jump to Review" anchors
5. **Product Reviews** — Individual product cards (see below for format)
6. **Comparison Table** — All products side-by-side with scores
7. **Decision Guide** — "Best for [scenario]" picks
8. **How to Choose** — Education section (5-8 topics)
9. **FAQ** — 5-8 questions with answers
10. **Final Verdict** — One-sentence summary + top pick
11. **Related Articles** — 3-5 internal links

### Product Card Format

```
### [Product Name]
**🏆 Best Overall**  |  **Rating: 8.5/10**
- **Price:** $XXX
- **Key Spec:** Value
- **Key Spec:** Value

**Pros:**
- Pro 1
- Pro 2
- Pro 3

**Cons:**
- Con 1

[Review paragraph — 100-150 words of personal experience / research]

**[👉 Check Price on Amazon]**
[📖 Read Full Review →] (if applicable)
```

### Topic Selection

- **Pillar topics:** "Best [Category] for [Year]" — broad, high-volume
- **Cluster topics:** "Best [Category] for [Specific Use Case]" — long-tail, buyer intent
- **Supporting topics:** "How to Choose [Category]", "[Category] vs [Category]", "Are [Products] Worth It?"
- **Evergreen guides:** "[Topic] for Beginners", "Ultimate [Topic] Guide"
- **No fluff content:** Every article must serve a specific buying decision

### Keyword Targeting

- Primary keyword in H1 and first paragraph
- Long-tail keywords in H2s (decision segmentation)
- Question keywords in FAQ section (targets People Also Ask)
- Use "for [year]" in titles for freshness signal
- Include specific product names in H3s

## 3. Trust Building — The Most Critical Gap

### Required Trust Signals (all must be implemented)

| Signal | Priority | Implementation |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| **Author bylines** | 🔴 Critical | Every article needs a human author name, photo, and 2-3 sentence bio |
| **About page** | 🔴 Critical | Team story, mission, criteria, editorial standards, how we make money |
| **"Why Trust Us" box** | 🔴 Critical | Appears on every review article — 2-3 sentences about testing process |
| **Testing methodology** | 🟡 High | Dedicated section explaining how products are selected, compared, and scored |
| **Quantified claims** | 🟡 High | "Tested 15 products", "Researched 40+ hours", "Compared 7 criteria" |
| **Original photography** | 🟡 High | At minimum: product images (can be from manufacturer with attribution), site screenshots |
| **Rating scores** | 🟡 High | 1-10 scale for each product on every review page |
| **Editor's notes** | 🟢 Medium | "Updated July 2026" callout boxes at top of updated articles |
| **Affiliate disclosure** | ✅ Already present | Maintain current disclosure language |
| **Last updated dates** | ✅ Already present | Maintain current date display |

### Scalable Photography Strategy (Without Buying Everything)

Since we can't buy every product initially:

1. **Manufacturer images** — Use Amazon product images with proper attribution. Amazon associates can use product images legally.
2. **Category hero images** — Create original branded graphics (canva-style) showing category concepts
3. **Selective purchasing** — Buy 1-2 top products per category for real photography
4. **Borrowing program** — Friends, family, reader submissions
5. **Comparison setups** — When you own 2+ products in a category, photograph them together
6. **Infographics** — Custom comparison graphics that are brandable and shareable

### Author Strategy

Since Temple Keep is a single-person operation:
- **Use real name:** "Mike Franzoni" or a brand name like "Temple Keep Team"
- **Author photo:** A professional-looking headshot
- **Author bio:** "Mike is a health researcher and product reviewer focused on non-toxic living. He's been researching water quality, air purification, and supplement science since 2025."
- **Consistency:** Same author name on every article builds recognition
- **Transparency:** If not all products are personally tested, say so: "I've personally tested 3 of these 7 products and researched the remaining 4 extensively."

## 4. Conversion Optimization

### Affiliate Link Placement

| Location | Type | Priority |
|----------|------|----------|
| Product card title | Direct product link | ✅ Already done |
| "Check Price on Amazon" button | Styled CTA button | 🔴 **Needs to add** |
| Comparison table | Affiliate link per row | ✅ Already done |
| "Best For" pick | Affiliate link | ✅ Already done |
| Quick picks section | Affiliate link | 🟡 **Needs to add** |
| FAQ section where relevant | Inline affiliate link | 🟢 Nice to have |

### CTA Button Design

Replace text product links with styled buttons:
- **Color:** Green (#2d6a2d) or gold (#d4a853) — consistent with site theme
- **Text:** "👉 Check Price on Amazon" or "👉 View on Amazon"
- **Placement:** After every product review, in comparison table, in quick picks
- **Size:** Prominent but not obnoxious — inline with content
- **Hover:** Subtle lift effect

### Comparison Table Improvements

Add to every review article:
- Column for **Rating (1-10)** — calculated from criteria scores
- **Sortable** or at minimum visually scannable
- **Price column** with dollar amounts
- **Key Spec column** (most important differentiator)
- **CTA column** with "View on Amazon" link

## 5. SEO Strategy

### Schema Markup (Priority Order)

| Schema Type | Where | Impact |
|-------------|-------|--------|
| **Product** | Each product review | Rich results with price, rating, availability |
| **Review** | Each product card | Star ratings in SERP |
| **FAQPage** | FAQ section | Expandable FAQ in SERP |
| **Article** | Entire article | ✅ Already implemented |
| **BreadcrumbList** | Navigation | Breadcrumb in SERP |
| **HowTo** | Buying guide sections | Step-by-step SERP display |
| **Organization** | Site-wide | ✅ Already implemented |
| **Person** | Author bio | Author authority in Google's eyes |

### Internal Linking Strategy

- Every article links to 5-10 other related articles
- Category pages link to all articles in that category
- Pillar pages link to cluster articles
- Cluster articles link back to pillar pages
- Use keyword-rich anchor text (not "click here")
- Breadcrumbs on every page

## 7. AI Workflow vs. Human Workflow

### What AI Does Well
- Content structure templates
- Product specification aggregation
- Comparison table generation
- FAQ drafting
- Schema markup generation
- SEO optimization (titles, headings, meta descriptions)
- Internal linking suggestions
- First draft of education sections

### What Human Must Do
- Personal experience anecdotes
- Original photography
- Methodology decisions
- Final editorial review
- Fact-checking claims
- Testing products
- Reader engagement
- Author bio authenticity

### The "Human Touch" Rules

1. **Never publish AI output as-is** — Every article gets a human review pass
2. **Add one personal anecdote per article** — "I've been researching water filters for 8 months and here's what I found..."
3. **Use "I" language** — Not "one should" but "I recommend"
4. **Cite real sources** — Link to studies, NSF certifications, manufacturer specs
5. **Be honest about limitations** — "I haven't personally tested this one, but based on specs and reviews..."
6. **Update frequency** — Refresh articles every 3-6 months with new products and info
